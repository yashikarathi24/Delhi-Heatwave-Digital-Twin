# Relative Humidity (2 m)

## Why Relative Humidity?
- Measures moisture in the air.
- Affects human heat stress.
- Used with temperature to estimate apparent temperature.
- Standard meteorological variable.

## Why not Dew Point?
- Not available in IMDAA.
- More useful for condensation studies.

## Why not Specific Humidity?
- Harder to interpret.
- Not available in IMDAA.

## Dataset Information

Variable Name: 2r

Scientific Name: relative_humidity

Measurement Height: 2 metres

Units: %

Dimensions:
- Time
- Height
- Latitude
- Longitude

## Observations

- Humidity ranges approximately from 12% to 43%.
- Air is relatively dry.
- Humidity increases from west to east in this snapshot.

## Conclusion

Relative Humidity is an important feature because it influences heat stress when combined with air temperature.
