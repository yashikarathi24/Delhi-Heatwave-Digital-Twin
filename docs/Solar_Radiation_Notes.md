# Solar Radiation

## Why Solar Radiation?

- Primary source of surface heating.
- Directly influences land surface temperature.
- Major driver of heatwave hotspot formation.

## Why Mean Surface Downward Shortwave Radiation Flux?

- Measures solar energy reaching the Earth's surface.
- Represents actual heating of roads, buildings, and land.
- More suitable than radiation at the top of the atmosphere.

## Why not TOA Radiation?

- Measured before sunlight passes through the atmosphere.
- Does not represent energy reaching the ground.

## Dataset Information

Variable Name:
msdwswrf

Full Name:
Mean Surface Downward Shortwave Radiation Flux

Units:
W/m²

Dimensions:
- Time
- Latitude
- Longitude

## Observations

- Higher values indicate more solar energy reaching the surface.
- Areas receiving more solar radiation are more likely to develop heat hotspots.
- Solar radiation becomes nearly zero during nighttime, which is physically expected.

## Conclusion

Solar Radiation is one of the most important meteorological variables because it provides the energy that heats urban surfaces and contributes to heatwave hotspot formation.
